Building AI that derives answers through verifiable logic, not pattern matching.

The Problem
Current AI hallucinates. High-stakes decisions can't.
Every major LLM is a pattern-matching engine trained to sound right. In casual use, that's fine. In legal, medical, compliance, or financial contexts, where a wrong answer has real consequences, it's a liability.
⚠️

Hallucination is structural, not a bug

Language models generate plausible text. They don't verify it. When a question requires multi-step logical reasoning, they guess, confidently and incorrectly.

🔍

No traceability

You can't audit how a chatbot reached its answer. In regulated industries, that's a non-starter. You need to show your work, not just produce an output.

🔌

Not built for your workflow

General-purpose AI isn't designed to plug into domain-specific pipelines. You need something that takes your rules, your documents, your facts, and reasons over them reliably.

⚡

Lightweight by design

General-purpose LLMs need hundreds of billions of parameters to store world knowledge AND reason. Our engine strips away everything except logic. A 1.88B parameter model focused purely on reasoning. No memorized facts, no language overhead. Just logic.

Why It Works
Trained to reason, not to remember

The White Room Engine was trained entirely on an invented alien vocabulary, symbols with no connection to any human language. The model had no choice but to learn the abstract logical structure of problems, because there was nothing to memorize.

Every training example was generated and verified by formal solvers, the same class of software used to prove mathematical theorems. Zero label errors. Zero ambiguity. The model learned what correct reasoning actually looks like.

The result is a reasoning core that transfers cleanly to real-world domains. When you feed it your rules and facts, it applies the same rigorous inference. Not pattern-matching dressed up as logic.
